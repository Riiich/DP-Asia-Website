DigitalPersona Compliance Solutions - CA SB1386

California Breach Disclosure Act (SB 1386)

The California Breach Disclosure Act (CA 1798.82), also known as SB 1386 requires State Agencies and private businesses to report information security breaches that potentially compromise personal information. Failing to comply can result in civil or class action lawsuits. The law covers not only California-based agencies, but all private enterprises doing business in California. The law requires that customers be notified if any unauthorized individuals have acquired their personal and/or financial information, thereby giving them the opportunity to take proactive steps to ensure that they do not become victims of identity theft.

Similar laws exist in other U.S. states, in Europe (EU Directives),and in Canada (PIPEDA).

Identity Compliance and Privacy Laws

Managing identity compliance is fundamental to meeting privacy law requirements and preventing breaches in the first place. DigitalPersona's products are critical in complying with the following requirements from general audit guidelines :

Strong Passwords

  • Establish a password management process that encourages users to protect passwords.

Secure Communications:

  • Encrypt sensitive customer information transmitted electronically.

Multi-Factor Authentication

  • Ensure confidentiality of personal data; use strong authentication to ensure that no one except designated employees have access to personal data.

Transaction Accountability

  • Prevent tampering using strong authentication
  • Enable non-repudiation of transactions involving personal data.
