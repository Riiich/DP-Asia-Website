DigitalPersona Compliance Solutions - GLBA

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)

The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) requires financial services institutions and universities to develop precautions to ensure the security and confidentiality of customer records and information, to protect against any anticipated threats or hazards to the security or integrity of such records, and to protect against unauthorized access to, or use of, such records or information which could result in substantial harm or inconvenience to any customer. Customer information includes for example, social security numbers, bank account numbers, credit card account numbers, date and/or location of birth, account balances, payment histories, credit ratings, income histories, driver's license information, Automated Clearing House (ACH) numbers, and tax return information. Many financial institutions use FFIEC (Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council) guidelines as a framework to comply with the IT security requirements of GLBA.

Identity Compliance and GLBA

Managing identity compliance is fundamental to meeting GLBA requirements. DigitalPersona's products play a critical role in complying with the following "Physical and Technical Safeguards" requirements:

Strong Passwords

  • Use password activated screensavers.
  • Use strong passwords that change periodically; prevent passwords from being written down.
  • Limit access to customer information to employees who have a business need to see it.
  • Store electronic customer information on a secure server accessible only with a password or other control.

Secure Communications

  • Encrypt sensitive customer information transmitted electronically.
