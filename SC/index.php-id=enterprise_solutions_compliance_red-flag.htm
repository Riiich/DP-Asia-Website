DigitalPersona Compliance Solutions - Red Flag

Red Flag Rules Act

The Federal Trade Commission and the National Credit Union Administration established the Red Flag Rules Act as part of the Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions (FACT) Act of 2003. Red flag rules apply to financial institutions and creditors like banks, credit unions, auto dealers, mortgage brokers, utility companies and telecommunications companies. The deadline for the program was originally November 1, 2008, but in October 2008, the Federal Trade Commission extended the deadline for compliance with the guidelines until May 1, 2009.

The act requires financial institutions and creditors to develop and implement written identity theft prevention programs. The programs must provide for the identification, detection, and response to patterns, practices, or specific activities known as Red flags that could indicate identity theft.

The Red Flag Rules identity theft prevention program must be approved by the board of directors, and administered by a designated senior management employee. In addition, the company must assign specific responsibility for implementation, train staff, audit compliance, generate annual reports and oversee anyone granted access to covered accounts.

The Red Flags include: alerts, notifications or other warnings received from consumer reporting agencies, notices from consumers, victims of identity theft or law enforcement officers, suspicious documents such as forgeries or a photo description that does not match a person, suspicious personally identifying information (e.g., inconsistent or mismatched addresses, Social Security numbers, etc.); and other events that indicate a likelihood of an occurrence of identity theft.

Identified flags must be reviewed with respect to the types of accounts offered and maintained, the methods used to open and provide access to the accounts, and any previous experience with identity theft.

Identity Compliance and Red Flag Rules

Managing identity compliance is fundamental to meeting Red Flag requirements. Detecting Red Flags requires obtaining identifying information about, and verifying the identity of, persons opening covered accounts and having a process to authenticate customers, monitor their transactions and verify the validity of change-of-address requests. Properly responding to potential Red Flag events requires proof of effective prevention and mitigation measures in place. Examples include monitoring covered accounts for evidence of identity theft, contacting the consumer, changing passwords or security codes, refraining from collecting on an account or selling it to a debt collector, or notifying law enforcement.

To address these requirements, DigitalPersona's solution provides:

Strong passwords

  • Identify all users with a unique user name before allowing them to access any systems containing the identity of persons opening covered accounts.

Secure Communications

  • Safeguard transmission of sensitive data during transmission over open, public network to transact covered account activity outside the organization.

Multi-factor authentication

  • Provide multi-level verification of a user's identity, going beyond strong passwords, and providing high levels of accountability

Transaction Accountability

  • Authenticate transactions by assigning a unique ID for all access to any database containing covered account information including transactional access by applications, administrators, and all other internal users.
  • Generates granular user authentication event logs for use by monitoring and reporting tools for auditing purposes.
